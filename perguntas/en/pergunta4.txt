📌 Pergunta:

When making a purchase in SAP Business One, what is the recommended logical sequence of documents (Best Practice)?

🔘 Alternatives:

A) A/P Invoice → Payment → Purchase Order.

B) Purchase Order → Goods Receipt PO → A/P Invoice. ✅

C) Payment → A/P Invoice → Purchase Order.

D) Goods Receipt PO → Purchase Order → Payment.

E) A/P Invoice → Goods Receipt PO → Purchase Order.

F) Purchase Order → Payment → Goods Receipt PO.

✅ Correct response: B

✔ Justification: B
Alternative B reflects the standard Purchasing (A/P) flow:
1. **Purchase Order**: Commitment to the supplier.
2. **Goods Receipt PO (GRPO)**: Physical entry into inventory.
3. **A/P Invoice**: Financial and accounting record.

❌ SE O USUÁRIO ERRAR:

👉 Se responder A:
❌ Incorrect
You are recording the debt before ordering the product. In SAP B1, the ideal flow starts with the request/order.

👉 Se responder C:
❌ Incorrect
Paying before recording the invoice does not follow the system's standard reconciliation flow.

👉 Se responder D:
❌ Incorrect
The receipt should be based on a previously approved order to ensure verification.

👉 Se responder E:
❌ Incorrect
The Invoice usually ends the physical entry flow, it doesn't start it.

👉 Se responder F:
❌ Incorrect
Although it's possible to pay in advance, the standard "Best Practice" for the inventory flow is to receive before invoicing.

🎁 BÔNUS:
💡 In SAP B1, you can use the "Document Generation Wizard" to copy information from the Order to the Receipt with one click (Copy To button). This avoids typos and saves a lot of time!
